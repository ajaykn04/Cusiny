Butter Chicken

500g chicken, cut into pieces
2 tablespoons butter
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 cloves garlic, minced
1 tablespoon ginger, minced
2 tomatoes, chopped
1/2 cup cream
1/2 cup yogurt
2 tablespoons tomato paste
1 tablespoon curry powder
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
Fresh cilantro for garnish

1. Heat butter in a pan, add onions and cook until golden brown.
2. Add garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute.
3. Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
4. Stir in curry powder, turmeric, and garam masala.
5. Add chicken pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides.
6. Stir in yogurt, cream, and tomato paste.
7. Cook for 20 minutes, until chicken is cooked through.
8. Season with salt to taste.
9. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Gulab Jamun

1 cup milk powder
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1/4 cup ghee
1/4 cup milk
1/4 teaspoon baking powder
Oil for frying
1 cup sugar
1 cup water
1/2 teaspoon rose water

1. In a bowl, mix milk powder, flour, baking powder, and ghee. Gradually add milk to form a smooth dough.
2. Shape the dough into small balls.
3. Heat oil in a pan and fry the balls until golden brown.
4. For the syrup: Combine sugar and water in a pan and bring to a boil. Simmer for 5 minutes and add rose water.
5. Soak fried balls in warm syrup for at least 1 hour before serving.

Mango Lassi

1 cup plain yogurt
1 cup ripe mango, peeled and chopped
1/2 cup milk
2 tablespoons sugar
A pinch of cardamom powder
Ice cubes

1. In a blender, combine yogurt, mango, milk, sugar, and cardamom powder.
2. Blend until smooth.
3. Add ice cubes and blend again.
4. Serve chilled.

Paneer Tikka

250g paneer, cut into cubes
1/2 cup plain yogurt
1 tablespoon lemon juice
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1 teaspoon paprika
1 teaspoon garam masala
1 tablespoon vegetable oil
Salt to taste
Skewers

1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, cumin powder, paprika, garam masala, and oil.
2. Add paneer cubes and marinate for at least 2 hours.
3. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
4. Thread the marinated paneer onto skewers.
5. Place skewers on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until paneer is slightly charred.
6. Serve hot with mint chutney.

Vegetarian
===============================================================================================
Raita

1 cup plain yogurt
1 cucumber, grated
1 tomato, finely chopped
1 small onion, finely chopped
2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
Salt to taste

1. In a bowl, whisk yogurt until smooth.
2. Add grated cucumber, chopped tomato, onion, and cilantro.
3. Stir in cumin powder, chaat masala, and salt.
4. Mix well and refrigerate until ready to serve.
